## The Core Chain

Use this chain when judging a company:

`ordinary people -> latent/scattered demand -> coordination and trust -> demand certainty -> supply reform -> lower structural cost and better quality -> joy/social experience -> long-term public institution`

If a move only touches price, subsidies, traffic acquisition, or GMV, it is a weak match. If it changes the demand/supply/trust structure, it may be a strong match.

## Company Alignment Test

Score each dimension as `strong / mixed / weak / unknown`.

| Dimension | Strong Huang Zheng Fit | Weak Or False Fit |
|---|---|---|
| User definition | Serves broad ordinary users and recognizes many scenes within one person. | Labels users as "low-end", "traffic", or monetizable clicks. |
| Demand structure | Aggregates scattered, similar, or time-flexible demand into useful certainty. | Merely copies group-buying, coupons, or referral tactics. |
| Supply impact | Lowers middle costs, improves quality, enables factories/farms/producers to match real demand. | Price is subsidized without structural cost improvement. |
| Experience | Combines value-for-money with joy, interaction, and reduced decision burden. | Purely transactional, addictive, or noisy without consumer benefit. |
| Trust/governance | Makes good sellers and quality products more likely to win. | Allows counterfeits, fake orders, merchant abuse, or internal corruption to scale. |
| Competition | Finds a new scene/dimension and creates incremental value. | Fights empire-style for the same territory, exclusivity, or vanity share. |
| Openness | Creates optionality for merchants/users/partners where possible. | Replaces one lock-in with another lock-in. |
| Time horizon | Accepts short-term expense/noise to build intrinsic value. | Optimizes quarterly optics, market cap, or founder reputation. |
| Institution | Builds governance, succession, partner mechanisms, and social responsibility. | Depends on founder heroics or personal color. |

## CEO Counterfactual

If Huang Zheng were CEO, infer likely priorities in this order:

1. **Find the overlooked scene.** He would ask where incumbents are trapped by old success: search, traffic, SKU, channel, empire, or finance logic.
2. **Start from people, not category labels.** He would reject simplistic user classes and look for multiple scenes within the same person.
3. **Turn demand into certainty.** He would design a product mechanism that turns patience, social connection, shared interest, location, or timing into batchable demand.
4. **Use certainty to reshape supply.** He would push direct producer access, flexible production, agricultural/industrial efficiency, or fewer middle layers.
5. **Make value felt.** He would care whether users feel a real surplus: cheaper than expected, better than expected, easier than expected, or happier than expected.
6. **Systematize trust.** He would invest in rules, anti-counterfeit, anti-corruption, and algorithmic governance before bad actors define the platform.
7. **Avoid the founder stage.** He would make the organization more institutional, younger, more transparent, and less dependent on him.
8. **Communicate with long-term owners.** He would explain roughness honestly, emphasize intrinsic value, and invite investors to accept uneven short-term metrics.

## Latest-Move Diagnosis

When the user asks whether a latest move fits Huang Zheng logic:

1. Verify current facts from primary/current sources.
2. Identify the move type: product, pricing, supply chain, logistics, overseas, governance, AI, agriculture, factory/C2M, marketplace rules, capital allocation, or leadership.
3. Map the move to graph nodes:
   - Product/social: `people_first`, `pin`, `costco_disney`.
   - Supply chain/agriculture/factory: `demand_side_reform`, `c2m`, `semi_market_supply`.
   - Platform rules: `benfen`, `good_currency_loop`, `anti_counterfeit`.
   - Competition/market entry: `offset_competition`, `unique_existence`, `open_ecosystem`.
   - Leadership/governance: `public_institution`, `succession`, `social_responsibility`.
4. Ask the decisive question: **Does this move create a self-reinforcing loop of consumer value, producer value, and platform trust?**
5. State what evidence would change the conclusion.

## Red Flags

Treat these as evidence of deviation unless there is strong contrary proof:

- Growth depends mainly on paid traffic, coupons, or subsidies without cost-structure improvement.
- Users are segmented with status contempt rather than understood as people with different scenes.
- The company describes social features, but the mechanism does not create demand certainty or trust.
- Platform rules let bad merchants, fake goods, fake reviews, or internal rent-seeking grow faster than good actors.
- "Low price" is achieved by quality sacrifice, supplier squeeze, hidden fees, or regulatory arbitrage.
- Expansion is justified by empire logic: own every layer, block partners, force exclusivity, or attack incumbents head-on without a new scene.
- Management optimizes market cap, founder image, or quarterly profit at the cost of intrinsic value.
- The founder remains the only immune system; governance and succession do not mature.
